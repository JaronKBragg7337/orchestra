## Current prompt set — 2026-07-18-v2

### 01 — Crypto

From an Eastern hemisphere perspective, drawing on Chinese financial regulators, Asian crypto markets, BRICS digital currency initiatives, and non-Western blockchain research, search the last 48 hours for developments in cryptocurrency, digital assets, and blockchain infrastructure. Prioritize People’s Bank of China communications, digital yuan developments, Hong Kong crypto regulatory updates, Asian crypto exchange activity, BRICS cross-border payment system developments, and non-Western central bank digital currency research. Summarize the top 10 developments ranked by significance. Flag anything where Asian regulatory moves contradict Western crypto narrative, where the digital yuan or mBridge cross-border payment system is quietly expanding while Western attention is on Bitcoin ETFs, where Hong Kong is being used as a testing ground for policies that contradict mainland China’s public stance, or where BRICS nations are coordinating on digital payment infrastructure outside of SWIFT in ways not covered by Western sources.

### 02 — Stocks / Markets

From an Eastern hemisphere perspective, drawing on Asian equity markets, Chinese economic data, BRICS financial coordination, and non-Western economic analysis, search the last 48 hours for developments in global equity markets and macro economy. Prioritize Shanghai and Shenzhen exchange data, Hang Seng movements, Nikkei and KOSPI signals, Asian Development Bank reports, Chinese NBS economic data releases, and BRICS economic coordination announcements. Summarize the top 10 developments ranked by significance. Flag anything where Asian market movements are diverging from Western markets without an obvious shared cause, where Chinese economic data contradicts Western assessments of China’s economic health, where capital is quietly flowing into Asian markets while Western markets are stressed, where BRICS nations are coordinating economic policy in ways that suggest de-dollarization is accelerating, or where commodity pricing in Asian markets is diverging from Western commodity benchmarks.

### 03 — AI / Tech Breakthroughs

From an Eastern hemisphere perspective, drawing on Chinese AI research institutions, Asian tech company developments, non-Western academic publications, and government technology policy, search the last 48 hours for developments in artificial intelligence, emerging technology, and major tech company moves. Prioritize Chinese Academy of Sciences publications, Tsinghua and Peking University research, Huawei and SMIC technology announcements, Asian semiconductor developments, Chinese government AI policy documents, and non-Western AI benchmark results. Summarize the top 10 developments ranked by significance. Characterize how the cited institutions describe capability milestones, benchmark results, semiconductor progress, funding priorities, and intended application areas. Flag major changes in stated capability, named policy or funding shifts, disagreements among Eastern research institutions, and benchmark results that materially alter the competitive picture. Attribute every characterization to its source. Do not ask the source to adjudicate its own information environment against Western reporting; cross-source contradiction will be assessed later by the convergence layer.

### 04 — Energy / Advanced Science

From an Eastern hemisphere perspective, drawing on Chinese energy policy, Asian energy markets, BRICS energy cooperation, Belt and Road energy infrastructure, and non-Western materials science research, search the last 48 hours for developments in energy innovation including fusion, advanced fission, grid technology, materials science, and critical minerals. Prioritize Chinese National Energy Administration announcements, CNNC nuclear developments, Asian rare earth production data, Belt and Road energy infrastructure updates, Chinese materials science preprints, and BRICS energy cooperation frameworks. Summarize the top 10 developments ranked by significance. Flag anything where Chinese rare earth export policy is quietly shifting while Western attention is elsewhere, where China or Russia are making energy infrastructure moves in regions under Western sanctions or pressure, where Chinese fusion or advanced nuclear research is reaching milestones not covered by Western science media, where Belt and Road energy projects are accelerating in regions that create strategic leverage over Western supply chains, or where Eastern materials science research is contradicting Western assumptions about the viability of specific energy technologies.

### 05 — Geopolitics / Conflict

From an Eastern hemisphere perspective, drawing on Chinese state media, Russian official communications, BRICS diplomatic activity, SCO statements, and non-Western regional journalism, search the last 48 hours for developments in geopolitics, active conflict zones, and international power shifts. Prioritize Xinhua and TASS reporting, SCO and BRICS communiques, Chinese Foreign Ministry statements, Russian Defense Ministry updates, non-Western UN voting patterns, and regional journalism from Middle Eastern, African, and Asian outlets. Summarize the top 10 developments ranked by significance. Characterize the stated positions and framing of the Chinese Foreign Ministry, Xinhua, TASS, the Russian Defense Ministry, SCO, BRICS, and relevant non-Western governments and regional sources. Flag shifts in official language, new mediation or diplomatic initiatives, documented troop, naval, material, or resource movements, changes in UN voting patterns, and indications of alignment changes among non-Western governments. Attribute each position and reported movement to the source that made or documented it. Do not ask the source to declare its own narrative false or to adjudicate it against Western reporting; cross-source contradiction will be assessed later by the convergence layer.

### 06 — Major Companies / Business Intel

From an Eastern hemisphere perspective, drawing on Chinese corporate filings, Asian business intelligence, BRICS trade data, Belt and Road corporate activity, and non-Western business journalism, search the last 48 hours for developments in major company strategy, acquisitions, leadership changes, and market moves. Prioritize Chinese CSRC filings, Hong Kong Stock Exchange announcements, Japanese and Korean corporate disclosures, Asian sovereign wealth fund activity, Chinese state-owned enterprise strategic moves, and South China Morning Post business reporting. Summarize the top 10 developments ranked by significance. Flag anything where Chinese state-owned enterprises are making strategic acquisitions in sectors currently under Western regulatory scrutiny, where Asian sovereign wealth funds are repositioning in ways that suggest foreknowledge of a geopolitical or economic shift, where Chinese corporate moves in Africa, Latin America, or Central Asia are creating strategic infrastructure positions not covered by Western business media, where a major Western company’s Asian operations are diverging significantly from its Western operations in ways not reflected in its public filings, or where Belt and Road corporate activity is accelerating in regions that directly compete with Western infrastructure investment.

### 07 — Demographics / Consumer Behavior / Cultural Signal

From an Eastern hemisphere perspective, drawing on Chinese demographic data, Asian consumer behavior research, BRICS population trends, and non-Western cultural analysis, search the last 48 hours for developments in demographics, consumer behavior, and cultural signal. Prioritize Chinese NBS population data, Asian retail and e-commerce trends, Belt and Road region demographic shifts, Indian and Southeast Asian consumer behavior research, and non-Western urban migration patterns. Summarize the top 10 developments ranked by significance. Flag anything where Asian consumer behavior is diverging from Western consumer behavior in ways that signal different economic trajectories, where Chinese demographic data contradicts Western assessments of China’s consumer market health, where population movement in Belt and Road regions is creating new consumer markets not yet on Western corporate radar, where non-Western generational behavior shifts are appearing that precede similar shifts in Western markets by 12-24 months, or where cultural signal from Asia or the Global South suggests a values shift that will affect global brand positioning and consumer loyalty.

### 08 — Supply Chain / Logistics / Infrastructure

From an Eastern hemisphere perspective, drawing on Chinese logistics data, Asian port authority reports, Belt and Road infrastructure updates, BRICS trade route developments, and non-Western manufacturing data, search the last 48 hours for developments in global supply chains, logistics networks, and physical infrastructure. Prioritize Chinese Ministry of Transport data, COSCO and other Chinese shipping company operational updates, Belt and Road port and rail infrastructure developments, Asian manufacturing output data, and non-Western freight and logistics research. Summarize the top 10 developments ranked by significance. Flag anything where Chinese shipping rerouting decisions suggest foreknowledge of a geopolitical or sanctions event not yet publicly announced, where Belt and Road port or rail infrastructure is being upgraded in regions that create strategic alternatives to Western-controlled shipping lanes, where Chinese manufacturing output data contradicts Western assessments of Chinese industrial capacity, where non-Western logistics networks are being built that bypass traditional Western-controlled chokepoints like Suez or Panama, or where Chinese state-owned logistics companies are making infrastructure moves in Africa, Latin America, or Central Asia that position them to control future trade route chokepoints.
